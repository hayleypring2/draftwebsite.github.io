About

Prior to coming to the Blavatnik School, Pepper Culpepper taught at the European University Institute and at the Harvard Kennedy School. His book Quiet Politics and Business Power: Corporate Control in Europe and Japan (Cambridge University Press, 2011) was awarded the 2012 Stein Rokkan Prize for Comparative Social Science Research.

He is the author of Creating Cooperation (Cornell University Press, 2003) and co-editor of Changing France (with Peter Hall and Bruno Palier, Palgrave, 2006) and The German Skills Machine (with David Finegold, Berghahn Books, 1999).

His work has appeared in Comparative Political Studies, International Organization, Politics & Society, Socio-Economic Review, World Politics, West European Politics, and other leading journals.

He has published commentary in the Washington Post, Le Monde, International Herald Tribune, and The New Republic. A former Marshall Scholar at the University of Oxford, he has held long-term visiting appointments in France, Germany, and Japan.
